# REQ-018 — Tamper-evident disturbance and event records, ≥ 7 years

**Source:** §5.5 (p.3) of TSO-HVDC-2026-0142, Rev. B
**EARS type:** ubiquitous

## Proposed response

The Station writes all disturbance recordings, protection events, and
operator actions to a station historian protected by an append-only
audit log with HMAC chaining, providing end-to-end tamper-evidence:
any modification to any prior record breaks the chain, and the break
is detectable at any time via the station administrator's audit-verify
workflow. Records are retained for one (1) year online and for the
balance of seven (7) years in archival storage on the TSO's chosen
internal storage tier.

The disturbance-record schema follows the IEC 60255-24 (COMTRADE)
profile and covers all protection events, all setpoint applications
(REQ-023), all manual operator actions, and all alarm-handling
events. The schema is open to extension during the design phase if
specific TSO event categories are not already covered.

Verification: a tamper-evidence verification dry run will be included
in the factory acceptance pack — a deliberate alteration of an
archived record will be shown to be detected by the audit-verify
workflow, and the audit log of that verification itself will be
presented to the TSO's auditors. Independent assurance of the HMAC
chain has been provided on two prior projects by the TSO's own
cybersecurity team.

Comply.
