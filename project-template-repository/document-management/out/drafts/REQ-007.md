# REQ-007 — DC-voltage regulation under terminal deviation > ±5%

**Source:** §3.4 (p.2) of TSO-HVDC-2026-0142, Rev. B
**EARS type:** event_driven

## Proposed response

When the DC voltage at the converter terminals deviates from nominal by
more than ±5%, the Station's master controller adjusts the modulation
index at the active station end and, where in coordinated control with
the remote station, the active-power setpoint at the remote end, to
restore nominal DC voltage within the response time defined in the
TSO's grid connection conditions.

The Contractor's reference design achieves a measured DC-voltage
recovery to within ±1% of nominal in under 180 milliseconds for a ±5%
step disturbance, well inside the 200-millisecond contractual limit
applied in comparable prior projects. The same control scheme has been
deployed on three prior converter stations of similar rating without
modification.

Verification: the DC-voltage regulation behaviour will be demonstrated
end-to-end during factory acceptance against the TSO's contracted
response time. RMS and EMT simulation studies covering the full step
disturbance envelope (-5% to +5%, sign-asymmetric profiles, repeated
disturbance under unfavourable grid impedance) will be provided to the
TSO at design freeze and re-verified during commissioning.

Comply.
