# REQ-012 — Ride-through of a single converter submodule failure

**Source:** §4.4 (p.2) of TSO-HVDC-2026-0142, Rev. B
**EARS type:** unwanted_behavior

## Proposed response

If a single converter submodule fails during operation, the Station's
master controller activates the next available redundant submodule in
the same arm in under five (5) milliseconds, with no operator
intervention and no step change in transmitted active power. The
failed submodule is isolated by its local bypass and is replaceable
during the next scheduled outage window.

The Station is supplied with installed redundancy of **N+8 submodules
per arm** as standard. This figure is the Contractor's reference value
based on three prior comparable projects; the contractual minimum is
open in TSO-HVDC-2026-0142 (see the clarification flagged in the
Quality Analysis) and the Contractor recommends formalising N+8 as the
contractual minimum in the response phase.

Verification: a deliberate submodule trip will be staged during
factory acceptance and the activation of the redundant submodule will
be measured. The Contractor reports that two such events occurred on
the prior reference station during the first eighteen months of
operation; both were transparent to the TSO control centre and to
downstream electricity-market actors.

## Compliance position

Comply, with a contractual clarification requested on the minimum
redundancy level (proposed N+8 per arm).
