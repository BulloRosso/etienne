## EU-Wide Regulations (Applicable in All Member States)

These regulations apply to every battery placed on the EU market, regardless of destination country.

### EU Battery Regulation 2023/1542

**Status:** In force, phased implementation through 2031.

| Requirement | Effective Date | Applies To | Description |
|-------------|---------------|------------|-------------|
| General safety & labeling | 2024-02-18 | All batteries | CE marking, hazard symbols, capacity/voltage labeling, collection symbol |
| Carbon footprint declaration | 2025-02-18 | EV batteries | Declare carbon footprint class on label and in passport |
| Digital product passport | 2027-02-18 | EV batteries, industrial >2 kWh | QR code linking to digital passport with chemistry, capacity, durability, recycled content |
| Due diligence (supply chain) | 2025-08-18 | All operators | Supply chain due diligence for cobalt, lithium, nickel, graphite |
| Recycled content declaration | 2028-08-18 | EV, industrial, SLI | Declare % of cobalt, lithium, nickel, lead recovered from waste |
| Recycled content minimums | 2031-08-18 | EV, industrial, SLI | Min 16% cobalt, 6% lithium, 6% nickel from recycled sources |
| Performance & durability | 2027-08-18 | EV batteries | Minimum state-of-health requirements after defined cycles |
| Collection targets | 2027-12-31 | Portable batteries | Member states must collect 63% of portable batteries by weight |

**Impact on quoting:**
- All EV and industrial battery quotes must include Digital Product Passport (F-010) from 2027 onward.
- Carbon footprint data must be available — Enhanced Passport (F-010) recommended for EV applications.
- Supply chain documentation adds 1–2 weeks to first-order lead time for due diligence review.

### CE Marking — Low Voltage Directive 2014/35/EU & EMC Directive 2014/30/EU

All battery cells and modules placed on the EU market require CE marking. Included as standard in all EuroBatt products.

### RoHS Directive 2011/65/EU

Restriction of hazardous substances. All EuroBatt cells comply. Relevant substances: lead (<0.1%), cadmium (<0.01%), mercury (<0.1%).

### REACH Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006

Chemical registration requirements. EuroBatt maintains REACH compliance for all electrolyte and electrode materials. SVHC (Substances of Very High Concern) declarations available on request.

### ADR / UN 38.3 — Transport Regulations

All lithium-ion cells require UN 38.3 testing for transport classification. Included as standard. Packing instructions per ADR/IATA:
- PI 965 / PI 966 / PI 967 (lithium-ion cells and batteries)
- Section II for cells ≤20 Wh, Section IB for cells >20 Wh

## Country-Specific Requirements

### Germany (DE)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Batteriegesetz (BattG) — replaced by BattG2, effective 2024 | UBA (Umweltbundesamt) | Registration with stiftung ear / BattG-Melderegister before placing batteries on market | Mandatory. EuroBatt is registered. Customer must also register if they are the "first placer" in DE. |
| ElektroG (WEEE transposition) | stiftung ear | Applies to batteries in electrical equipment | Customer must register electrical equipment containing our cells. |
| VerpackG | LUCID / Zentrale Stelle | Packaging registration for transport packaging | EuroBatt handles packaging registration for shipments within DE. |
| Gefahrgutbeauftragtenverordnung (GbV) | BAM | Dangerous goods safety advisor required for shipping lithium batteries | EuroBatt has appointed DG advisor. Customer needs their own for re-shipping. |

**Labeling (DE-specific):**
- German-language hazard warnings mandatory on consumer-facing products.
- BattG return/collection symbol (crossed-out wheelie bin) required.
- If Cd >0.002% or Pb >0.004%: chemical symbol must appear below the bin symbol. (Not applicable to standard EuroBatt cells.)

**Additional notes:**
- Germany enforces strict producer responsibility. Quote should note customer's registration obligations.
- German market requires all documentation (SDS, test reports) to be available in German.

### France (FR)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Code de l'environnement, Art. L541-10-1 | ADEME | EPR (Extended Producer Responsibility) — registration with eco-organism (Corepile or Screlec) | Customer must register. Registration fee applies per kg of batteries placed on market. |
| Triman logo | — | Mandatory sorting logo on products/packaging sold to consumers | Required for B2C products. Include on label if customer sells to consumers. |
| AGEC Law (Anti-Waste Law) | — | Repairability index for certain electronic products containing batteries | May apply depending on customer's end product. |

**Labeling (FR-specific):**
- French-language warnings mandatory.
- Triman logo (sorting instruction) mandatory for consumer products.
- "Ne pas jeter au feu" (do not throw in fire) warning.

**Additional notes:**
- France applies an environmental contribution (éco-contribution) per battery placed on market: ~€0.02–€0.06/unit depending on chemistry and weight. This is the customer's obligation, not included in EuroBatt pricing.
- French customs may request REACH/CLP documentation in French.

### Italy (IT)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| D.Lgs. 188/2008 (Battery Directive transposition) | MATTM / ISPRA | Producer registration with CDCNPA (Centro di Coordinamento Nazionale Pile e Accumulatori) | Customer must register as producer. |
| EPR obligations | CDCNPA | Financing of collection and recycling via consortium membership | Customer obligation. Costs ~€0.03–€0.08/unit. |

**Labeling (IT-specific):**
- Italian-language safety warnings.
- Crossed-out wheelie bin symbol.
- Producer identification (name/trademark + registration number) on cell or packaging.

**Additional notes:**
- Italy requires a fiscal representative for non-Italian companies placing batteries on the market.
- Environmental compliance documentation (MUD report) required annually.

### Spain (ES)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Real Decreto 106/2008 (Battery Directive transposition) | MITERD | Producer registration with autonomous community registry | Customer must register in the relevant autonomous community. |
| EPR | Ecopilas / ERP España | Membership in authorized collection system | Customer obligation. |

**Labeling (ES-specific):**
- Spanish-language safety warnings.
- Standard EU collection symbol.

**Additional notes:**
- Spain has a fragmented registry system (per autonomous community). Customer should verify registration requirements for their specific region (Catalonia, Madrid, etc.).

### Netherlands (NL)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Regeling afgedankte batterijen en accu's | RWS (Rijkswaterstaat) | Producer registration with Stichting Batterijen or ARN (for automotive) | Customer must register. |
| EPR | Stichting Batterijen | Collection and recycling financing | €0.02–€0.05/unit depending on type. |

**Labeling (NL-specific):**
- Dutch or English warnings acceptable for industrial products.
- Standard EU labeling requirements.

**Additional notes:**
- Netherlands is generally pragmatic with enforcement. English documentation accepted for industrial/B2B applications.

### Sweden (SE)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Förordning (2008:834) om producentansvar för batterier | Naturvårdsverket | Producer registration | Customer must register. |
| EPR | El-Kretsen / Recipo | Collection system membership | Customer obligation. |
| Chemical tax (kemikalieskatt) | Skatteverket | Tax on electronics containing certain flame retardants | May apply to battery modules with FR-4 PCBs in BMS. Check per configuration. |

**Labeling (SE-specific):**
- Swedish-language warnings for consumer products. English acceptable for industrial.
- Standard EU symbols.

### Poland (PL)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Ustawa o bateriach i akumulatorach (2009) | GIOŚ (Chief Inspectorate of Environmental Protection) | Producer registration in BDO (Baza danych o odpadach) | Customer must register. BDO number required on invoices. |
| EPR | Authorized organizations | Collection financing | Customer obligation. |

**Labeling (PL-specific):**
- Polish-language warnings mandatory for consumer products.
- BDO registration number must appear on documents.

**Additional notes:**
- Poland requires the BDO registration number on all commercial documents (invoices, delivery notes) for battery transactions.

### Austria (AT)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Batterienverordnung (BattVO) | BMK / ERA (Elektroaltgeräte Koordinierungsstelle) | Producer registration with ERA | Customer must register. |
| EPR | ERA | Collection system financing | Customer obligation. |

**Labeling (AT-specific):**
- German-language warnings (same as DE).
- Standard EU symbols.

**Additional notes:**
- Austrian requirements closely mirror German requirements. Documentation prepared for DE market is generally accepted in AT with minor adjustments.

---

### Belgium (BE)

| Regulation | Authority | Requirement | Impact |
|------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| KB/AR (Royal Decree) on batteries | Bebat | Producer registration with Bebat | Customer must register. |
| EPR | Bebat | Collection and recycling | €0.03–€0.10/unit. |
| Language requirements | — | Labeling in Dutch, French, and German (all three national languages) | Trilingual labeling for consumer products. |

**Labeling (BE-specific):**
- Trilingual warnings (NL/FR/DE) mandatory for consumer products.
- Bebat logo on consumer batteries.

**Additional notes:**
- Belgium's trilingual labeling requirement is the most complex in the EU. Plan label design accordingly.
- Bebat provides a "Bebat contribution" scale based on battery weight.
